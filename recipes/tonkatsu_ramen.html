Tonkatsu Shoyu Ramen

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ½ teaspoons spicy sesame oil
  • 2 (1x3-inch) boneless pork chop slices
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 tablespoon black sesame seeds, divided
  • 2 leaves fresh basil, chopped, divided
  • 1 leaf fresh sage, chopped, divided
  • 1 (3 ounce) package instant ramen noodles (such as Shirakiku®), seasoning packet discarded
  • 3 tablespoons shredded dried kombu
  • 1 ¾ tablespoons bonito soup stock (such as Hondashi®)
  • 1 ¾ tablespoons soy sauce (shoyu)
  • ½ teaspoon white miso paste with dashi

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Gently lower the egg into the boiling water and cook for 6 minutes. Drain the egg and immediately place it in a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process. Once cooled, peel the egg and set it aside.
  2. In a shallow dish, combine the spicy sesame oil, pork chops, and 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Toss to coat the pork evenly. Marinate for 15 minutes or up to an hour.
  3. Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the marinated pork chops and cook for 3-4 minutes per side, or until cooked through. Sprinkle with half of the black sesame seeds.
  4. Divide the ramen noodles into two serving bowls. Set aside.
  5. In a medium saucepan, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the dried kombu, bonito soup stock, soy sauce, and miso paste. Stir until the miso paste is dissolved. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Divide the hot broth evenly between the two serving bowls. Add the ramen noodles and cook for 1-2 minutes, or until softened.
  7. Top each bowl with the cooked pork chops, halved egg, chopped basil, chopped sage, and remaining black sesame seeds.
  8. Serve immediately and enjoy!
